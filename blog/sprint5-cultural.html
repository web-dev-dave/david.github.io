Core skills reflection

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

In the last 5 weeks I have learnt that I love a challenge. I love coming from a place of 'not knowing' and through my hard work and effort moving to a place of 'knowing'. Establishing new chemical pathways and consistent new learning stimulates me and keeps my mind sharp.

I learnt that control of ego (or lack of) can dictate our growth. We can fall in to patterns which fuel our ego that can be a blessing or a curse to our ability to move through life. Through this course/readings and interactions with others have I been more aware of the impact our ego plays in life.

I learnt that making smaller goals to meet at the beginning the day helps with my feeling of accomplishment at the end of the day. If I focus on meeting a goal that is too great to achieve that can make the day feel wasted if I do not meet it. Breaking down a big goals in to smaller ones reminds me that I am progressing.

What are the role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?

Nothing is more powerful than surrounding yourself in a positive enviroment with similair indivduals who are striving to learn and to help one another on your learning journey. Being able to connect through values, empathy and self awarness can we unlock invaluable tools to further our growth and the growth of others.

Holding the same values or beliefs we are able to find common ground and are able to communicate easier with one another. Showing empathy and understanding that others might be struggling we can support and further their growth and in turn furthers our own growth, teaching others is a great way of reinforcing your knowledge on the area. Being self aware we can monitor our learning and change accordingly if we can see something is working or something is not working. Being able to be self critical and open to criticism allows for reflection and the door to moving forward.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning and what was the most challenging?

It has been the most challenging yet most rewarding side of foundations. Initially I struggled with it and found it hard to switch from writing and learning the technical side of web development to then jumping ship and writing blogs on the core side. But as the weeks went on I found it easier to explore and understand that both the technical and core learning go hand in hand, and reflecting on oneself and being self aware can help us push past our limitations and reach new heights.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

We are heavily reliant on computers and writing in code that it can look like to be the best developer we must 'become' a computer or understand every inner working of code and I think it's important to remember that this is not the case. It's more important that we develop human and core skills to attach that to our technical skills. I want to be technically profecient at coding but I also want others to be able to see that there is values, beliefs and emotion attached to what I do and how I work. In a team enviroment we need to be able to be relatable and so I think thats why its so important to learn and teach these skills.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practicing programming instead?

Not at all. I think as you can read from my previous reflections that it is vitally important to strengthen that side of us. In a world where technology is evolving in a rapid rate it is learning and growing our core side that helps us remain 'human'. Bringing our indentity in to our work I believe will make it more approachable and better recieved than work that has no values, beliefs or emotion attatched to it.
